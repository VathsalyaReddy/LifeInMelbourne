From Mumbai (or Chennai, or Bangalore) to Melbourne

Moving to Melbourne as an Indian woman is an experience full of contrasts — the quiet of leafy suburbs after the chaos of Indian cities, the politeness that sometimes feels distant, the freedom that takes getting used to, and the small joys of finding a good masala chai in an unexpected café.

This space is about the real, unfiltered moments — the homesickness, the wins, the confusion, the laughter, and everything in between.

Life Here, One Chapter at a Time

🍛

Finding Desi Food

From biryani in Dandenong to dosas in the CBD, Melbourne's Indian food scene is surprisingly rich. Harris Farm Markets and Indian grocery stores like Bharat Emporium keep the pantry stocked with everything from hing to jaggery.

🚃

Navigating Public Transport

The myki card, trams, trains, and buses — Melbourne's PT network is a lifeline. Once you master the zones and the free tram zone in the city, getting around becomes second nature.

🌦️

Four Seasons in One Day

Melburnians aren't joking about the weather. Packing a jacket, umbrella, and sunscreen in the same bag is standard practice. The city's unpredictable skies are part of its charm.

🎓

Student & Work Life

Whether studying at Melbourne Uni, RMIT, or working in tech, healthcare, or hospitality — balancing studies, casual work, and building a career here comes with its own journey and lessons.

🏡

Finding a Home

The rental market can be tough. From inspections to references and bond payments — finding a comfortable place in suburbs like Clayton, Glen Waverley, or Footscray is an adventure in itself.

🤝

Building Community

Joining cultural associations, attending Garba nights, Diwali events, and weekend cricket matches — the Indian community in Melbourne is vibrant, welcoming, and always evolving.

Hey, I'm the girl behind this blog

I moved to Melbourne chasing a dream — of better opportunities, broader horizons, and yes, maybe a little adventure too. What I found was so much more: a city that challenges you, surprises you, and slowly, quietly, wins you over.

This blog is my little corner of the internet where I share the highs and lows of life as an Indian woman in Melbourne. From navigating the rental market to finding the perfect chai latte, from homesickness to belonging — I write it all here.

If you're planning to move to Melbourne, already here and figuring things out, or just curious about what life is like down under — welcome. Pull up a chair. Let's talk.
